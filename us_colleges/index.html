Applying to US universities

Testing

These can be divided into there

  • Standardized tests
  • Language Tests
  • Subject Tests

An international applicant especially one from a national curriculum is encouraged to take the standardized tests.

The most common standardized tests are the ACT and the SAT.
Other tests include SAT Subject Tests, the TOEFL (Test of English as a Foreign Language) Duolingo Tests and the IELTS.

The ACT is scored out of 36. The higher the score the better as all things academic go!

SAT Reasoning Test is scored out of 800 in 2 sections, summing up to 1600 as a perfect score. Preferably a score of 1400 and above.

SAT Subject Tests – they are subject based on different subject areas from Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, Biology to World History et cetera. Also scored out of 800.

TOEFL - Test of English as a foreign language It is given on different versions (computer based, or paper based),
I advise one to apply with a score of 100 and higher out 120
You can also convert from different types of the test to compare your scores

Grades and Transcripts

You'll need school transcript for at least the last two years of highschool They'll have to be certified by the school.
Remember

  • These are to uploaded by a college counselor. You will have to submit them to him/her for them to be uploaded.
  • A college counselor is an overseer or the key person in charge of your communications between colleges and you
  • In some cases, the counsellor is a designated official, but in some cases a teacher in school (preferably the Academic Master) may assume the role

Essays

They are a key and central part of your application.

There are often prompts, which in will try to ask you to share about yourself

Visit the common app site for essay prompts

When it comes to preparing for the essay prompts, there are several online resources to aid on that
A good example is the college essay guy site

Extra-curriculars

Activities that a student does to supplement thier academics in the process of being a “well rounded student”.
Advisably, the range and mix of activities can be;

Services
Any thing related to giving (leadership, mentoring, charity, volunteering and many others)
Creativity
Centered on self growth. Includes singing, debating, researches that are not in the school’s syllabus system, artworks (drawings, paintings), Programming.
Action/Sports
Physical endurance activities. Includes soccer, basketball, track and field, tennis, volleyball and many others
Initiatives
Starting up something on your own (a daring attempt as such would set a difference to standout as an applicant). It may be a club at school, a business, a tuition center, a website or blog , a non profit or for profit organization. It can be on any range (as small as a school, as large as a town or country network
Jobs
If you have ever had a job whose experience has been significant to your life.

Letters of recommendations

These are letters which other people write about you.
You may and should communicate with them what to share beforehand, but you can not have access to what they write about after they submit it
You would often require a minimum of 2 recommendation letters. Atleast one from two subject teachers and one from your college counselor
It is advised to be a mix from different subject areas such as one letter from a Math/Science Teacher, and another letter from a Language/Humanities teacher!

Interviews

• They are arranged by the admission offices • They are often arranged after you submit your application. If you can meet the interviewer in person, it is highly recommended, but in most cases for international applicants it is done over Skype or in any form of online video or audio chat. • The key to interviews is enjoying the conversations and being honest. Answer the questions in detail and be confident. Don’t be overwhelmed, don’t over or under prepare. BE YOURSELF once again • Always be on time and neat in appearance.

Financial Aid

Applying for financial Aid for international students is often done via the CSS (College Scholarship Service) profile on the college board website. Every financial Aid applicant will require to submit some form of of income.
This can be as a

  • A copy of tax forms of two years before. For example if you are applying for financial Aid for the year 2022/23, you need to have your parents or guardian full annual tax forms for the year 2018. These can be a letter from a parents employer indicating the Parents Annual incomes and taxes or Parent’s annual bank statement. Samples needed here!
  • If one's parents or guardians are self employed, a copy of their bank statement or tax information are to be used!

Often top colleges offer enough aid to support your learning and whilst being fair to the applicant’s family. You may need to visit college specific websites and communicate with their offices of admissions on their Financial Aid policies and how much aid you can expect depending on your financial profile
